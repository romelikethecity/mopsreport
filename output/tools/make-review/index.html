Make (formerly Integromat) Review for Marketing Operations (2026)

Overview

Make is a visual integration platform that sits between Zapier (simplicity) and Workato (enterprise power). Its visual workflow builder and per-operation pricing make it popular with cost-conscious MOps teams.

In our analysis of 295 marketing operations job postings, Make (formerly Integromat) appears in 2 listings (0.7% of all tracked roles). It represents a notable skill within the integration and automation category.

Key Features

  • Visual scenario builder with drag-and-drop modules
  • 1,500+ app integrations
  • Data transformation with built-in functions
  • Error handling with retry and fallback routes
  • Webhooks and HTTP modules for custom connections
  • Data stores for persistent state between runs

Pricing

Free (1,000 ops/mo), Core $9/mo, Pro $16/mo, Teams $29/mo, Enterprise by quote

Pricing for Make (formerly Integromat) varies based on your team size, contact volume, and selected modules. Contact Make (formerly Integromat) directly for a custom quote based on your requirements.

Pros

  • Visual builder makes complex workflows transparent
  • Per-operation pricing is cheaper than Zapier at volume
  • Better error handling than Zapier
  • Good balance of power and usability

Cons

  • Smaller integration library than Zapier
  • Less enterprise-grade than Workato
  • Community is smaller, fewer tutorials available
  • Some modules lack depth compared to native integrations

Who Should Use Make (formerly Integromat)?

Make (formerly Integromat) is best suited for MOps teams at mid-market to enterprise organizations that need robust integration and automation capabilities. If you are evaluating Make (formerly Integromat), compare it against the alternatives in our Integration and Automation category.
